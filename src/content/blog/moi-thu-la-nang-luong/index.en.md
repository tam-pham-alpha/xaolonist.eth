Hey, it's him again: a scientist, a curious soul. He often asks questions and experiments on himself. He is always obsessed with Einstein's quote: "Everything is energy and that's all there is to it. Match the frequency of the reality you want and you cannot help but get that reality. It can be no other way. This is not philosophy. This is physics." He is wondering if there is a divine connection between brain energy, quantum entanglement, and karma

A question keeps hovering in his mind: Why does the brain, which accounts for a mere 2% of body mass, consume 20-25% of total energy when the body is at rest? Stranger still, when the mind operates at full capacity: thinking, imagining, creating: the energy consumption is only slightly higher than when it is relaxed. So, what is the majority of brain energy used for?

Perhaps the brain does not only use energy to run thoughts and control the body, but also to connect and communicate with the universe

When we think, when an idea sparks, when an emotion flares up: we might have unconsciously transmitted energy signals, tiny but never disappearing into the void. Those signals resonate, entangle, and connect with other existing energy sources, with people, with things

These invisible connections can transcend space and persist through time, lasting much longer, perhaps even longer than the life of the person who created them. Those interwoven networks of energy, the threads of cause and effect we spin daily, or the beautiful resonances meeting at the right time, inadvertently affect our future selves

Could it be that our every thought, emotion, and action never truly ends? They are simply sending out energy waves, drawing invisible currents in the vast ocean of the universe
