Hey, it's him: Chi, having just finished a bowl of onion porridge, turning to discuss knowledge

Could it be that knowledge is becoming so cheap that ordinary men like him accidentally acquire the secret weapon to access the entirety of human wisdom? He is a superhero, he thinks

Is it true that the universe will always send lessons to humans, until they finally learn them? Knowledge is now flattened, like gold in the '54s. Yet, only those who accept their own imperfections and dare to explore the mysteries of the universe will multiply that knowledge fivefold or tenfold. Otherwise, they remain ordinary men drowned in chaotic ignorance, thinking they grasp the secrets of the universe: just like him

Is it true that when the fire era ends, humanity will experience not only a wealth gap, but also a cognitive stratification? As written in the Bible: "For to everyone who has will more be given, and he will have an abundance: but from the one who has not, even what he has will be taken away"

And is it true that eventually we will realize that within GOLD lies GOD, and at the end of the path of knowledge, where humans spend their brief lifetimes searching, the Creator is waiting?
