Hey hey, it's him again..., something has been on his mind for quite a while.

He used to not understand why Vingroup was willing to burn billions of dollars on electric vehicles. He also did not understand why the state poured so much policy support into VinFast. Looking at the financial statements, it was a massive investment, even a reckless one.

## A new era taking shape

But the more he watched the world, the more he felt this was not only Vingroup's story. It looked like a decision born from an entire era.

In Xuan Kong Flying Star there is the idea of **Period 9**, also called the **Li Fire Period**. Believers say this is the age of electricity, energy, AI, media, and new technology industries. He does not know how true that is. What caught his attention is that if you set feng shui aside completely, the world is still pouring money into exactly those fields.

Tesla bet on EVs. NVIDIA bet on AI. TSMC bet on chips. BYD bet on batteries. OpenAI bet on artificial intelligence. Very different companies, in very different countries, looking in the same direction. In Vietnam, perhaps no company has bet harder than Vingroup.

Ten years ago, people saw Vingroup as a real-estate conglomerate. Ten years from now, they may remember them more for industry and technology. At least, that is the path they are throwing so much of their resources into.

## Cash flow is the real answer

Maybe Vingroup does not care what the Li Fire Period is. They simply believe the world will electrify. If that belief is right, what they are building today becomes the foundation for decades. If it is wrong, it will be one of the most expensive gambles in Vietnamese corporate history.

But the more he thought, the more he saw that the most important question was not whether VinFast would succeed. It was whether Vingroup had enough resources to wait for VinFast to succeed. Because seeing the future and getting there have never been the same thing.

For many years, real estate was almost the cash-flow engine of the group. That cash flow fed long-horizon bets like EVs. If real estate stayed healthy, they bought more time. But if the old cash flow slowed while the EV industry still needed huge capital, pressure would appear. Not because the direction was wrong, but because the future often arrives slower than cash flow can endure. That is also what he finds most interesting about investing.

A trend can be right. A vision can be right. And a company can still fail if it runs out of money before the future arrives. Seeing correctly is not enough. You have to live long enough to be proven right. Then he suddenly understood something else...

## The all-in of a lifetime

He used to wonder why the state paid VinFast so much attention. Now he has another way to see it. Perhaps what needed protecting was not only one company, but Vietnam's chance to step into a high value-added industry. If a domestic firm can compete at global scale, the value it creates goes far beyond that firm's own profits.

That does not guarantee VinFast will succeed. But it partly explains why the state has incentive to walk with the EV industry. Perhaps that is why VinFast's story is no longer only the story of a car brand. It is a test of whether Vietnam can use cash flow from the old cycle to buy a ticket into the new one.

If it works, people will call it vision. If it fails, people will call it a gamble. But both names begin from the same decision.

Perhaps the Li Fire Period does not even exist. But every era has a "period" of its own. Not fate written in the sky, but the fate of capital flows, of technology, and of how people create value.

And perhaps what decides the final win is not who saw the future earlier. It is who still has enough resources to walk until that future becomes the present.
